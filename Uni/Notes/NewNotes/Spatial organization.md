#### **Aggregation**
Moves the individual robots to congregate spatially in a specific region of the
environment. This allows individuals of the swarm to get spatially close to each other for further
interaction.
![[Pasted image 20260217205302.png]]

#### **Pattern formation**
Arranges the swarm of robots in a specific shape. A special case is chain formation
where robots form a line, typically to establish multi-hop communication between two points.
![[Pasted image 20260217205344.png]]
![[Pasted image 20260217205407.png]]

#### **Self-assembly**
Connects the robots in order to establish structures. They can either be connected
physically or virtually through communication links. A special case is morphogenesis where the
swarm evolves into a predefined shape.
![[Pasted image 20260217205421.png]]
![[Pasted image 20260217205449.png]]


#### **Object clustering and assembly**
Lets the swarm of robots manipulate spatially distributed objects.
Clustering and assembling of objects is essential for construction processes
